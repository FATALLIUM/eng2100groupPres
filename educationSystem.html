Chapter: Reading and Writing are not Connected



Education system

“Even a two-year-old who has been read to consistently will recognize that “once upon a time” indicates the beginning of a story, and will often begin that same way when asked to make up his or her own.” (Carillo, 39)

“Colleges and universities are potentially producing students, or citizens, who think reading is passive. These students might blindly accept whatever comes their way rather than actively engaging ideas, asking questions, and seeking out multiple perspectives.” (Carillo, 39)


Throughout elementary school, students are taught writing and reading simultaneously as it is important to know both to understand the other. The ability to comprehend a text with writing is an essential skill needed to move forward.

As students leave elementary school and enter high school after a couple of years, they are taught something completely different about the connection of writing and reading. This causes students to struggle with their writing as they are no longer able to comprehend what they are reading.

Teachers and professors are upset by the poor writing skills of their students, but they don’t acknowledge that the student is struggling due to the lack of attention given to improving student’s ability to read.
