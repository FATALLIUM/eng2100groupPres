Chapter: Reading and Writing are not Connected



Writing and reading

“When one writes, one is creating meaning by putting words and ideas together.” (Carillo, 40)

“Students may lack the ability to read the world around them because they do not have the tools to recognize the values and assumptions that inform the images, advertisements, news stories, political campaigns, and ideas with which they come into contact on a daily basis.” (Carillo, 39)



Without the ability to read writings, texts may just seem to be a jumble of words that make sense but are not comprehended.

Writing allows the writer to express their feelings, thoughts, or share knowledge. Both reading and writing require creativity. You express emotion(s) through writing, and you experience emotion(s) through reading.
